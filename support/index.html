Reminder Creation

  • How do I create a new reminder?

    On iOS, tap the button at the bottom of the main card screen. On Mac, click the button at the top left of the window within the sidebar. You can also create reminders on iOS using the 3D touch shortcut or the share extension.

  • How do I edit a reminder?

    You can edit reminders from the schedule view. Tap or click the icon on the main card screen. On Mac, double click the desired reminder to edit. On iOS, tap the desired reminder to edit. You can also tap the reminder title on the main card screen to begin editing.

  • How do I delete a reminder?

    Open the schedule view from the icon on the main card screen. Locate the reminder to delete and tap or click the icon. Select the delete option.

  • How do I create a reminder with an alert before the due date?

    On the add screen, make sure you have the On a date option enabled.

    On iOS tap the date. On the date screen tap the Remind me button beneath the date picker. Select the amount of time you’d like to be reminded beforehand.

    On Mac click the button to the right of the date. Select the amount of time you’d like to be reminded beforehand.

    Reminders with early alerts show up in your stack before the due date.

  • How do I create a reminder without a notification?

    On the add screen, make sure you have the On a date option enabled.

    On iOS tap the date. Tap the Show notifications toggle to the off position.

    On Mac click the button to the right of the date. Click the Show notifications checkbox to the off position.

  • How do I create custom repeat schedules?

    On the add screen, make sure you have the On a date option enabled.

    On iOS tap the active repeat duration. Select the Custom option. To repeat on days of the week, make sure to use the week option.

    On Mac click the button to the right of the active repeat duration.

Reminder Actions

  • How do I complete a reminder?

    To complete the top card on iOS, swipe it up. You can also tap the button or use the schedule view. Tap the icon on the main card screen. Locate the reminder to complete and tap the icon. Tap the Complete button.

    To complete the top card on Mac, click the button. You can also complete reminders from the schedule view. Click the icon on the main card screen. Locate the reminder to complete and click the icon. Click the Complete button.

  • How do I snooze a reminder?

    To snooze the top card on iOS, swipe the card down. You can also tap the button. To bypass your default snooze setting, force press or long press the snooze button. Select between preset options or choose a specific date.

    To snooze the top card on Mac, click the button. To bypass your default snooze setting, right click the snooze button. Select between preset options or choose a specific date.

  • How do I skip a reminder in my stack?

    To skip the top card on iOS, swipe the card left or right. If VoiceOver is enabled, tap the button.

    To skip the top card on Mac, click the button. You can also two-finger swipe left or right using your trackpad, or scroll left or right using your mouse.

  • How do I sort or prioritize the reminders in my stack?

    Open the schedule view from the icon on the main card screen. On iOS, tap the button at the top right. Sort reminders in the “In Your Stack” section using the drag handles. You can also begin bulk editing by long-pressing on a reminder.

    On Mac, the drag handles appear when you hover over the reminder. Items appear in your stack from top to bottom.

iCloud Syncing

  • How do I enable or disable iCloud syncing?

    Tap or click the icon on the main card screen. On iOS, tap the iCloud toggle to enable or disable iCloud. On Mac, click the iCloud tab in the Doo Preferences window. Click the iCloud toggle to enable or disable iCloud.

    Doo requires an active iCloud account with iCloud Drive enabled. Read the official Apple instructions for step-by-step help enabling iCloud on your iOS device or Mac.

  • How can I force iCloud to sync?

    You can force syncing with iCloud by showing and hiding the Control Center on iOS. You can also force a sync by re-opening the application from the home screen.

    You can force an iCloud sync on Mac by clicking your desktop and then clicking inside the application window.

  • Why do I see the ‘iCloud Unavailable’ message?

    Doo is transparent about reporting errors, even ones that don’t affect syncing. Most issues are harmless and resolve themselves automatically.

    We take syncing issues seriously. If iCloud appears broken or inconsistent, please contact us for troubleshooting help. Be sure to include any error messages and possible ways to reproduce the issue.

  • What happens if I clear the iCloud cache?

    Doo uses a special iCloud feature to sync only the changes that have occurred since the last sync. The syncing mechanism stores a handful of references to remember when the last changes occurred. Resetting the iCloud cache deletes these references and will tell iCloud to sync all data during the next request. Use this feature to troubleshoot syncing issues.

Share Extension

  • How do I enable the Doo extension?

    On iOS:

    • Open Safari and make sure you’re on a webpage.
    • Tap the icon.
    • Beneath Airdrop in the first row of icons, swipe to the end of the list and tap the icon.
    • Scroll through the list of Activities until you find “Add to Doo”. Make sure the toggle is enabled. You can drag the Doo extension up in the list for easier access.
    • Tap the Done button.

    On Mac:

    • Open “System Preferences” from the Apple Menu.
    • Open the “Extensions” preference item in the third row of icons.
    • Make sure the “All” item on the left column is selected.
    • Find Doo in the list on the right.
    • Make sure Share Menu is checked.

    You can now add reminders using webpages, web URLs, selected text, and more wherever you see the icon.

  • Why did the extension tell me no text or URL could be found?

    The Doo extension can use text and web URLs. Some apps convert things like addresses into text that Doo can read. If an app attempts to send an object to Doo that it can’t read, you’ll see this message.

    Let us know if there’s a share menu that you think should work with Doo.

iOS Widget

  • How do I enable the Doo iOS widget?

    • Open the Search screen by swiping right on the home screen.
    • Scroll to the bottom of the Search screen.
    • Tap the Edit button.
    • Find Doo in the list of items and tap the icon.
    • Tap the Done button.

    The iOS widget displays up to five reminders. Tap an item to open Doo with that reminder’s card on top of your stack. Tap the app icon to open Doo.

  • The iOS widget doesn’t seem to load or takes a long time to appear.

    The Doo widget responds to loading notifications from iOS. On slower devices it’s not unusual for this process to take up to a minute.
